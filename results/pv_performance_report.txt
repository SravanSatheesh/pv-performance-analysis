PV Performance Analysis – Dortmund, Germany

Objective:
This project evaluates the solar photovoltaic energy generation potential in Dortmund using hourly solar irradiance data from PVGIS for the period 2015–2023.

Methodology:
The dataset was processed using Python (pandas, matplotlib). Hourly global irradiance values were converted into photovoltaic energy output using assumed panel
area (1.6 m²) and module efficiency (18%). Energy production was aggregated into daily, monthly, and annual values.

Results:
The analysis shows clear seasonal variation in PV production, with peak generation during summer months and minimal output during winter. Average annual PV energy 
production was estimated to be approximately XXXX kWh for the assumed system configuration.

Tilt angle sensitivity analysis indicated that panel inclination around 30° provides optimal energy yield for Dortmund’s geographic location.

Economic evaluation suggests that a small residential PV system can recover installation costs within approximately XX years under current electricity prices.

Conclusion:
The study demonstrates that solar PV systems are technically viable and economically beneficial for residential installations in Dortmund. The workflow also highlights 
the usefulness of data-driven analysis for renewable energy system planning.
